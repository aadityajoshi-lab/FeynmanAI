# Research provenance - Feynman Build Week submission

This sidecar records the learning-science sources used in `HACKATHON_SUBMISSION_FINAL.md` and the limited claim each supports.

| Product design choice | Research source | Supported framing | Deliberate limit |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Ask the learner to retrieve, predict, or explain before receiving the answer | [Roediger & Karpicke (2006), *Test-enhanced learning*](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/16507066/) | Retrieval/testing can improve delayed retention relative to repeated study in the reported experiments | Does not prove Feynman improves outcomes |
| Capture reasoning and uncertainty, not only a final answer | [Chi et al. (1994), *Eliciting Self-Explanations Improves Understanding*](https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/10.1207/s15516709cog1803_3) | Prompted self-explanation supported deeper understanding in the study context | Does not validate an LLM rubric or all domains |
| Route weak attempts to focused remediation rather than generic failure | [Kapur (2008), *Productive Failure*](https://doi.org/10.1080/07370000802212669) | Productive struggle before support can be beneficial in a designed learning sequence | Does not justify unbounded confusion or withholding help |
| Include changed-case/transfer tasks | [Barnett & Ceci (2002), *When and Where Do We Apply What We Learn?*](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/12081085/) | Transfer should be distinguished from simple recall or recognition | Does not establish that Feynman currently measures far transfer rigorously |

## Product evidence kept separate from research evidence

- Product validation comes from `outputs/final-browser-acceptance-report-2026-07-21.md`, not these papers.
- The local alpha was browser-tested with live Qwen/Fireworks provider behavior and reported 151 backend tests plus 58 frontend tests at the time of the final QA report.
- The writeup deliberately avoids claims of measured learning gains, mastery certification, or clinical decision support.
