## span-01 — The mass question

A plant's dry mass is made mostly from carbon-containing compounds built from carbon dioxide and water during photosynthesis.

## span-02 — Carbon dioxide supplies carbon

During photosynthesis, carbon dioxide enters leaves and supplies carbon atoms that become part of sugars.

## span-03 — Water is raw material

Water supplies hydrogen and oxygen atoms and is a raw material used to make sugars.

## span-04 — Light supplies energy

Light provides energy for photosynthesis; light is not converted into the plant's dry mass.

## span-05 — Minerals are a small fraction

Mineral nutrients from soil are essential for growth, but they account for only a small fraction of most plant dry mass.

## span-06 — Carbon is not primarily from soil

The carbon in a plant's sugars is not taken primarily from soil; it is fixed from carbon dioxide in air.

## span-07 — The transformation

Photosynthesis uses light energy to combine carbon dioxide and water into sugars, releasing oxygen as a by-product.

## span-08 — Matter versus energy

A useful explanation should distinguish matter inputs (carbon dioxide, water, minerals) from an energy input (light).
